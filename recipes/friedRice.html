Sichuan-styled Mala fried rice is a bold and flavorful dish that marries the comforting qualities of fried rice with the distinctive, numbing spiciness characteristic of Sichuan cuisine. The term "Mala" is derived from two Chinese characters: "ma," meaning numbing, and "la," meaning spicy. This distinctive flavor profile is achieved through the use of Sichuan peppercorns and dried red chilies, which provide a unique, tingly sensation on the palate. The dish typically includes a variety of ingredients such as scrambled eggs, diced vegetables, and sometimes meat or tofu, all stir-fried together with day-old rice to ensure the perfect texture.

Mala Fried Rice Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup chopped baby carrots
  • ½ cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced, or to taste (Optional)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups leftover cooked and chilled white rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil, or to taste

How to make Mala Fried Rice


  1. Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil and cook for 3-5 minutes. Stir in peas, then immediately drain in a colander.

  2. Heat a wok over high heat. Pour in vegatable oil, then stir in carrots, peas, and garlic; cook for about 30 seconds. Add egfs; stir quickly to scramble eggs with vegetables.

  3. Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with seasame oil and toss again.

  4. Serve hot and enjoy!
